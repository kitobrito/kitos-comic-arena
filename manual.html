Official Game Manual

Learn the systems before the systems punish you.

This manual explains how Comic-Arena works: the turn engine, energy flow, damage logic, role structure, and the core habits that separate random play from real strategy.

Overview Game Modes Match Structure Core Mechanics Combat System Roles Strategy Fundamentals Additional Notes

1. Overview

Comic-Arena is a turn-based competitive strategy game that blends elements of card battlers with tactical drafting systems. Unlike traditional card games, each “card” is a full character with a unique ability kit and its own miniature combat engine.

Players build teams, manage resources, and outmaneuver opponents in fast-paced matches where timing, target priority, and team composition decide who survives the final turn.

2. Platform Availability

  • Web browsers
  • Mobile devices

3. Game Modes

  • Ladder: Ranked mode for climbing, progression, and competitive match results.
  • Quick Game: Unranked mode for practice, testing, and mission progress.
  • Private Match: Directly challenge a specific player by name.
  • Bot Matches: During low-traffic periods, AI opponents may fill the queue to keep the game active.

4. Match Structure

  • Each player selects three characters.
  • Matches usually last between 3 and 10 minutes.
  • The game uses a free-pick system, so any available character can be chosen.
  • Victory Condition: Reduce all enemy characters to 0 HP before your own team is defeated.

5. Core Gameplay Mechanics

5.1 Turn System

  • At the start of each turn, players gain 1 random energy color for each living character on their team.
  • The player who goes first only gains 1 total energy on the opening turn.
  • Players spend energy to activate skills.
  • After actions are chosen, the turn resolves according to the game’s timing and interaction rules.

5.3 Actions Per Turn

Each character may use one skill per turn by default. Some abilities grant additional actions and let a character exceed that baseline under specific conditions.

6. Combat System

The combat system is the strategic center of Comic-Arena. Most matches are decided by how well you understand timing, protection layers, counters, and target access.

6.1 Skill Types

  • Damage
  • Healing
  • Control effects
  • Utility effects

6.2 Damage Classes

  • Physical
  • Energy
  • Mental

6.3 Damage Types

  • Normal: Reduced by mitigation effects.
  • Piercing: Ignores damage reduction.
  • Affliction: Ignores both damage reduction and destructible defenses.

6.5 Targeting Rules

  • Harmful skills target enemies.
  • Helpful skills target self or allies.
  • Some abilities can function as both.
  • Target types include self, single ally, single enemy, all allies, and all enemies.

8. Strategy Fundamentals

  • Manage energy efficiently and think one turn ahead.
  • Prioritize the right target instead of always hitting the easiest one.
  • Build teams around synergy, not just individually strong characters.
  • Time counters, reflects, protection, and burst windows carefully.
  • Adapt to the opponent’s plan instead of repeating the same sequence every match.

Strong players balance offense, defense, and control. The team that spends its resources more cleanly usually wins long before the final knockout appears on screen.

9. Additional Notes

Comic-Arena is designed to be fast-paced but strategically deep. Every decision matters: what energy you preserve, which skill you queue, when you reveal protection, and which character you leave exposed.

Mastery comes from learning both your own roster and the opponent’s likely response patterns. If you understand what your team threatens and what the enemy needs next turn, you are already playing a different game from most people in queue.
